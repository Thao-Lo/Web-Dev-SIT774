How A Microwave Works

What is a microwave?

A microwave oven uses electromagnetic waves (microwaves) to heat food.

How it works

  • A magnetron converts electricity into microwaves.
  • Microwaves enter the cooking chamber and bounce around.
  • They penetrate the food and target water, fat, and sugar molecules.

How food heats

  • These molecules vibrate rapidly.
  • The vibration creates heat that cooks the food from the inside.

Why It's Fast

  • It heats only the food, not the air.
  • Cooking is faster and more energy-efficient than a regular oven.

To Wrap Up

Microwaves heat food by targeting water, fat, and sugar molecules, causing them to vibrate and generate heat.

This process cooks the food quickly and efficiently, without heating the surrounding air.
